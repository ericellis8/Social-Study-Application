Break Dancing Review

Last Friday I attended the event “Breaking Down the MU” and it was a lot of fun. I really had no clue what to expect when I went there and felt a little out of place at the beginning as I enjoy hip-hop music but would never attend an event like this on my own.  I convinced my roommate to go along with me and he seemed to have a good time. As the event progressed I started to really enjoy it and was extremely impressed by the dancers.  It’s amazing some of the stuff they can pull off.  They danced a lot like the videos you see on YouTube, but in person it’s so much cooler.

The event was structured like a classic dance battle from the movies, a circle formed and then random (at least I think it was random) dancers would go out into the circle and breakdance. Each dancer got about 30 seconds inside the circle at a time.  They we’re all really talented and there wasn’t a single dancer that didn’t look like they belonged. The moves were very similar to the one’s we researched a few weeks ago, and I personally liked when the dancers would freeze and hold their position almost like they’re floating.  

It was interesting that the majority of dancers were Asian and not too many African Americans.  It’s most likely because of where we live and the population of African Americans here is not too large.  It does show though how hip-hop and breakdancing is stretching out to a wider range of people.  

The music at the event was pretty good, as it was a lot of remixes to songs I’ve heard before.  I thought they did a good job of mixing some new school with old school.  I personally enjoyed watching the dancers pull of some moon walking with mixes of Michael Jackson playing in the background.  

I was also surprised to see that it seemed like all the break-dancers knew each other. They were all supportive of each other and pushed one another. The dancing really brought them all together.

All in all, I’m very happy that I got to experience the event.  Without this class I would have never known about it. It’s always cool to experience new things in life and get out of my comfort zone. I wish I were half as talented as the dancers there.  Obviously it takes a ton of practice and dedication for them to be as good as they are.
